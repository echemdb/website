    Pt(110) - \(0.1 \; \mathrm{M}\) \(\ce{ HClO4 }\)

    echemdb identifier: sandbeck_2019_dissolution_2997_f1c_solid_green
    tags: BCV

    A cyclic voltammogramm for Pt(110) recorded in \(0.1 \; \mathrm{M}\) \(\ce{ HClO4 }\) at a scan rate of \(50 \; \mathrm{mV\,s^{-1}}\) from Figure 1 in D. J. Sandbeck et al. Dissolution of platinum single crystals in acidic medium. ChemPhysChem, 20\(22\):2997–3003, 2019.

    Further information

    The figure shows digitized data.

    The Pt(110) electrode was prepared by:
    * flame annealing in a butane torch, while glowing red hot for at least 30 s
    * cooling in Ar/H2 (ca. 40% H2) for 2 min
    * protecting surface with a drop of Ar/H2 saturated ultrapure water
