    Pt(111) - \(0.1 \; \mathrm{M}\) \(\ce{ HClO4 }\)

    echemdb identifier: gomez-marin_2012_surface_558_f1_black
    tags: BCV

    A cyclic voltammogramm for Pt(111) recorded in \(0.1 \; \mathrm{M}\) \(\ce{ HClO4 }\) at a scan rate of \(50 \; \mathrm{mV\,s^{-1}}\) from Figure 1 in A. M. Gómez-Marín et al. Pt\(111\) surface disorder kinetics in perchloric acid solutions and the influence of specific anion adsorption. Electrochimica acta, 82:558–569, 2012.

    Further information

    The figure shows digitized data.

    The Pt(111) electrode was prepared by:
    * Flame annealed.
    * Cooled in a hydrogen/argon atmosphere.
    * Transferred to the cell, protected by a drop of ultra-pure water saturated with these gases.

    Comment left by the curator on the published figure Focus on Pt oxidation, surface defects (at steps), and sulfate vs. perchlorate adsorption.
