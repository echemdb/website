    Pt(110) - \(0.5 \; \mathrm{M}\) \(\ce{ H2SO4 }\)

    echemdb identifier: gomez_1993_electrochemical_189_f1_solid
    tags: BCV

    A cyclic voltammogramm for Pt(110) recorded in \(0.5 \; \mathrm{M}\) \(\ce{ H2SO4 }\) at a scan rate of \(50 \; \mathrm{mV\,s^{-1}}\) from Figure 1 in R. G’omez et al. Electrochemical behaviour of platinum surfaces containing \(110\) sites and the problem of the third oxidation peak. Journal of Electroanalytical Chemistry, 354\(1\-2\):189–208, 1993.

    Further information

    The figure shows digitized data.

    The Pt(110) electrode was prepared by:
    * Annealed in gas-oxygen flame and subsequently cooled in air down to 200-300 degree celsius and then protected with a droplet of water in equilibrium with air.
